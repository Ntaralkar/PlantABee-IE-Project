Glossy Abelia

  • Plant Type: Shrub
  • Height: 2.5m
    Width: 2.5m
  • Flowering Season: Summer
  • Information: This shrub has multiple woody cane-like, reddish-brown arching stems that form an open vase habit that becomes rounded with age. It has dark glossy green leaves that turn bronze in autumn and clusters of white funnel-shaped flower appear during summer.
  • Climate: Cool, temperate, warm/humid, frost hardy
  • Rainfall: Moderately drought hardy
  • Aspect: Full sun, partial shade
  • Aspect: Deep well-drained loam

Cootamundra Wattle

  • Plant Type: Tree
  • Height: 7m
    Width: 5m
  • Flowering Season: Winter
  • Information: Acacia baileyana also known as Cootamundra Wattle is a small evergreen tree with domed canopy, it is fast growing but short lived (8 - 15 years). Widely planted as a striking ornamental in gardens, streetscapes and parks. It can be trimmed as a hedge and tolerates mild coastal exposure. It provides a nitrogenfixing low
  • Climate: Cool, temperate, frost hardy
  • Rainfall: 450 mm, drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Full sun, partial shade
  • Aspect: Well-drained to heavy wet soils

Currawang

  • Plant Type: Tree
  • Height: 10m Width: 8m
  • Flowering Season: Spring
  • Information: Acacia doratoxylon or the Currawang is a deciduous tree with a broad canopy that often has an irregular outline.A good garden and park tree and maintenance-free street tree. Good for farm shelter and shade. May be used as a specialty timber, with a medium to dark brown wood similar to that of the sought-after A. melanoxylon (Tasmanian blackwood), used in turnery and as a cabinet timber.
  • Climate: Temperate, hot/arid, frost hardy
  • Rainfall: 250 mm, drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Full sun
  • Aspect: Light sandy loams to heavy clays, pH <6.5-7.5

Native Hibiscus

  • Plant Type: Shrub
  • Height: 2.5m Width: 3m
  • Flowering Season: Spring, Autumn
  • Information: A hardy and easy care small shrub growing to around a metre and a half high and wide, it has mauve-purple large flowers from spring through to autumn. It is dense growing and suitable for low hedging and screening as well as containers. It responds well to a frequent light prune, which should be done after flowering, and it can be shaped to size. Frost hardy, it does well in cool temperate to sub-tropical climates, in well drained soils and sun to part shade.A very attractive 'hibiscus'-looking flower that provides bold, bright colour as a specimen garden plant.
  • Climate: Cool, temperate, warm/humid, hot/arid. Prefers hot dry summers, cool wet winters
  • Rainfall: 200 mm, very drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Sunny and protected from wind
  • Aspect: Well-drained various. Intolerant of water logging

Smooth-barked Apple

  • Plant Type: Shrub
  • Height: 20m Width: 12m
  • Flowering Season: Spring, Summer
  • Information: Handsome, erect fast growing  tree with slightly pitted smooth pink new bark. Better suited to larger gardens and parks. Will grow in most well drained soils. Has been known to lose branches in high winds. Angophora costata also known as Smooth-barked Apple is widely planted as a street tree and an ornamental feature in public gardens and urban open spaces. It would make a good pollen support in bee forage
  • Climate: Temperate, warm/humid,hot/arid, mildly frost hardy
  • Rainfall: 600 mm, drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Full sun
  • Aspect: Well-drained soils from skeletal sandy loam on sandstone to heavy clay

Coast Banksia

  • Plant Type: Tree
  • Height: 10m Width: 4m
  • Flowering Season: Winter, Autumn
  • Information: Banksia integrifolia, commonly known as coast banksia, is a species of tree that grows along the east coast of Australia. One of the most widely distributed Banksia species, it occurs between Victoria and Central Queensland in a broad range of habitats, from coastal dunes to mountains. This very versatile tree is a suitable street and specimen tree in larger gardens and parks. It is a good windbreak tree; its timber is non-durable and has an ADD of 560 kg/m3. The wood, which is difficult to dry due to shrinkage is an attractive reddish–brown with a prominent medullary ray; it is suited to cabinet work and turning.
  • Climate: Cool, temperate, warm/humid (southern)
  • Rainfall: 850 mm
  • Aspect: Full sun
  • Aspect: Well-drained, various, from sand to clay, pH < 7.5

Saw-tooth Banksia

  • Plant Type: Shrub
  • Height: 10m Width: 3m
  • Flowering Season: Spring, Winter
  • Information: Banksia serrata, commonly known as old man banksia, saw banksia, saw-tooth banksia and red honeysuckle, is a species of woody shrub or tree of the genus Banksia in the family Proteaceae. Banksia serrata is a great frontline coastal species, very resistant to wind and salt spray. It makes a useful addition to shelterbelts and as a specimen tree in larger domestic gardens and public parks. It is used for cut flowers and in floral art. It has dense wood for a banksia, at 720 kg/m3, reddish to purple with a prominent medullary ray.
  • Climate: Cool, temperate
  • Rainfall: 650 mm, drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Full sun
  • Aspect: Well-drained poor sandy soil

Hairpin Banksia

  • Plant Type: Shrub
  • Height: 2m Width: 2m
  • Flowering Season: Spring, Fall, Winter
  • Information: The hairpin banksia is a species of woody shrub, of the genus Banksia in the family Proteaceae, native to eastern Australia. Widely distributed, it is found as an understorey plant in open dry forest or heathland from Victoria to northern Queensland, generally on sandstone though sometimes also clay soils. This highly adaptable species can be grown in a broad range of conditions, from coastal exposure to inner city smog. It is a very useful garden addition and a good farm windbreak component. Flower spike is used in floral art.
  • Climate: Cool, temperate, warm/humid
  • Rainfall: 500 mm, drought tolerant
  • Aspect: Full sun to part shade
  • Aspect: Well-drained, light to moderately heavy soils. Acid to neutral pH, but will tolerate slightly alkaline
